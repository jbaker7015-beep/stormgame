# Population Simulation

# Philosophy

Population systems give emotional and strategic weight to storms and weather agencies.

Cities should feel alive and reactive.

The population system should support:
- evacuations
- panic
- disaster response
- economic impact

---

# Population Attributes

Cities contain:
- population density
- infrastructure quality
- preparedness level
- economic value

---

# Civilian Behavior

Civilians react to:
- warnings
- storm severity
- evacuation orders
- infrastructure failures

---

# Panic System

Panic increases during:
- severe storms
- failed warnings
- sudden storm escalation

High panic reduces evacuation efficiency.

---

# Evacuation System

Successful evacuations:
- reduce casualties
- improve agency reputation
- increase funding rewards

Late evacuations:
- increase casualties
- increase chaos

---

# Infrastructure Interaction

Population depends on:
- roads
- power
- hospitals
- communication systems

Infrastructure damage worsens disaster outcomes.

---

# Population Growth

Long-term systems may include:
- rebuilding
- economic recovery
- migration
- disaster-resistant development

---

# Agency Reputation

Weather agencies build trust through:
- accurate forecasts
- timely warnings
- successful evacuations

Poor performance reduces public trust.

---

# Long-Term Goals

The population system should make:
- destruction meaningful
- forecasting important
- emergency management rewarding

The player should feel the consequences of severe weather.
