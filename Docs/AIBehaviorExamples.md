# AI Behavior Examples

# Philosophy

AI should feel:
- believable
- adaptive
- atmospheric
- ecosystem-driven

AI should simulate natural weather behavior while remaining fun to play against.

AI should not rely on unfair advantages.

---

# Storm AI Behaviors

## Moisture Pocket AI

Behavior:
- seeks nearby humidity
- avoids dry regions
- prioritizes survival

Difficulty Scaling:
- higher difficulties optimize movement paths

---

## Updraft AI

Behavior:
- seeks heat zones
- generates instability
- expands vertically

---

## Thunderstorm AI

Behavior:
- targets unstable regions
- grows aggressively
- competes with nearby storms

---

## Tornado AI

Behavior:
- targets populated areas
- maximizes destruction
- absorbs debris
- avoids dissipation

Hard difficulty tornadoes:
- predict movement paths
- optimize destruction routes

---

## Hurricane AI

Behavior:
- remains near oceans
- absorbs warm water energy
- slowly expands

Higher difficulties:
- optimize landfall timing
- preserve storm strength

---

# Agency AI Behaviors

## Meteorologist AI

Behavior:
- monitors storm growth
- predicts evolution paths
- issues warnings

Difficulty affects:
- prediction accuracy
- reaction speed

---

## Emergency Manager AI

Behavior:
- evacuates cities
- deploys emergency resources
- reduces casualties

---

## Infrastructure AI

Behavior:
- reinforces vulnerable structures
- restores utilities
- protects critical systems

---

# Difficulty Scaling

## Easy
- slower decisions
- inefficient routing
- delayed warnings

---

## Medium
- balanced decisions
- average forecasting
- moderate optimization

---

## Hard
- predictive behavior
- optimized growth paths
- strong mitigation planning

---

## Extreme
- near-perfect optimization
- adaptive strategy
- advanced forecasting logic

Extreme AI should still obey game rules.

---

# Ecosystem Goals

The world should feel:
- alive
- competitive
- reactive
- unpredictable

AI should create emergent gameplay rather than scripted encounters.
