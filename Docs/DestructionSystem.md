# Destruction System

# Philosophy

Destruction should:
- feel earned
- scale naturally
- evolve with storm intensity

Early storms should feel weak.

Late-game storms should feel catastrophic.

---

# Damage Categories

## Wind Damage

Caused by:
- high wind speeds
- tornado rotation
- downdrafts

Effects:
- structural damage
- debris generation
- tree destruction

---

## Flood Damage

Caused by:
- rainfall
- storm surge
- overflowing rivers

Effects:
- road flooding
- infrastructure shutdown
- population displacement

---

## Lightning Damage

Caused by:
- electrical storms
- severe thunderstorms

Effects:
- fires
- power outages
- electrical failures

---

## Hail Damage

Caused by:
- severe thunderstorms

Effects:
- vehicle damage
- roof damage
- crop destruction

---

# Structural Integrity System

Buildings contain:
- health
- resistance
- material type
- flood resistance
- wind resistance

---

# Infrastructure Systems

Critical infrastructure includes:
- power grids
- hospitals
- roads
- communication towers

Infrastructure failure impacts:
- population safety
- evacuation speed
- agency efficiency

---

# Debris System

Destroyed objects create debris.

Debris may:
- increase damage
- become airborne
- amplify tornado intensity

---

# Escalation Philosophy

Damage should escalate gradually.

The player should visibly feel storms becoming more dangerous over time.

---

# Performance Goals

Destruction systems should:
- prioritize gameplay readability
- remain optimized
- avoid excessive physics calculations

Not every object needs full physics simulation.

---

# Long-Term Goals

The destruction system should support:
- large-scale disasters
- city-wide impacts
- persistent environmental damage
- ecosystem changes
