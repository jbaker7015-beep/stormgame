# Gameplay Systems

# Core Gameplay Loop

1. Spawn as weak atmospheric disturbance
2. Gather atmospheric resources
3. Increase instability and storm mass
4. Evolve into stronger weather systems
5. Choose storm specialization paths
6. Compete against storms and weather agencies

---

# Atmospheric Resource Systems

## Humidity
Primary growth resource.

Sources:
- oceans
- lakes
- rivers
- humid terrain

Uses:
- cloud growth
- rainfall
- storm sustainability

---

## Heat
Generates atmospheric lift.

Sources:
- sunlight
- cities
- warm terrain
- oceans

Uses:
- instability
- storm intensification

---

## Instability
Represents atmospheric imbalance.

Generated by:
- heat differences
- rising air
- atmospheric conflict

Required for:
- thunderstorm evolution
- severe weather development

---

## Wind Shear
Advanced atmospheric mechanic.

Controls:
- rotation
- supercell formation
- tornado potential

---

# Evolution System

Storms evolve through stages based on:
- stored energy
- instability
- humidity
- environmental conditions

Evolution should feel gradual and earned.

---

# Destruction System

Damage scales based on:
- storm size
- wind speed
- rainfall intensity
- debris
- lightning activity

Different storm types specialize in different damage styles.

---

# Population System

Cities contain:
- population density
- infrastructure
- economic value

Storm damage affects:
- casualties
- economy
- environmental conditions

---

# XP System

Players earn XP from:
- storm growth
- survival time
- environmental control
- destruction
- successful forecasting
- reduced casualties

XP unlocks:
- upgrades
- cosmetics
- new storm types
- advanced abilities

---

# AI Ecosystem

AI storms:
- gather resources
- evolve
- compete
- survive

AI agencies:
- forecast storms
- issue warnings
- reduce damage

---

# Match Flow

Typical match:
1. atmospheric formation phase
2. storm development phase
3. severe weather escalation
4. late-game catastrophic storms

Matches should naturally escalate in intensity.

---

# Terrain Interaction

Terrain affects storm behavior.

Examples:
- oceans boost humidity
- mountains create lift
- cities generate heat
- forests affect moisture

---

# Long-Term Gameplay Goals

- emergent gameplay
- ecosystem simulation
- strategic evolution
- dynamic weather interaction
- replayable matches
