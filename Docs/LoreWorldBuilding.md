# Lore and World Building

# Philosophy

StormGame is not fantasy.

The world should feel grounded, believable, and inspired by real Earth meteorology.

However, as storms evolve into extreme late-game forms, the atmosphere may become increasingly dramatic and exaggerated.

The tone should feel like:
- a living Earth
- a global weather crisis
- a dynamic atmospheric ecosystem

---

# Setting

The world contains:
- modern cities
- coastlines
- forests
- plains
- mountains
- deserts
- oceans

Weather systems evolve naturally within these environments.

---

# Atmospheric Instability Era

The game takes place during a period of increasing atmospheric instability.

Storms are becoming:
- stronger
- more unpredictable
- more destructive

Weather agencies are struggling to adapt.

---

# Storm Intelligence Concept

Storms are not magical creatures.

However, gameplay represents storms as semi-living evolving systems driven by:
- atmospheric energy
- instability
- environmental adaptation

The player experiences the storm from the perspective of the evolving weather system itself.

---

# Weather Agencies

Global weather agencies have developed advanced forecasting systems to combat increasingly severe storms.

Agencies compete to:
- save lives
- reduce destruction
- improve forecasting technology

---

# Civilian Population

Cities react dynamically to storms.

Populations:
- evacuate
- panic
- prepare
- rebuild

Public trust becomes important for weather agencies.

---

# Long-Term Lore Possibilities

Potential future systems:
- climate escalation
- global atmospheric anomalies
- mega storms
- long-term environmental changes
- persistent world weather history

---

# Narrative Philosophy

Narrative should remain:
- environmental
- emergent
- simulation-driven

The weather itself should create the stories.
