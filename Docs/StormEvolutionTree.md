# Storm Evolution Tree

# Philosophy

Storm evolution should feel:
- natural
- gradual
- atmospheric
- rewarding

Players should experience the full lifecycle of weather development.

**Evolving design:** Stage numbers below remain useful for **visual tiers**; **storm modes** (supercell, squall line, bow, hook, hail core) are defined in [StormSimulationVision.md](StormSimulationVision.md) and implemented in **S4** of [StormFeaturesImplementationPlan.md](StormFeaturesImplementationPlan.md).

---

# Base Evolution Path

## Stage 1
Moisture Pocket

Focus:
- absorb humidity
- absorb heat
- survive

---

## Stage 2
Warm Air Updraft

Focus:
- generate lift
- increase instability
- improve growth rate

---

## Stage 3
Cumulus Cloud

Focus:
- begin rainfall
- increase mass
- expand absorption

---

## Stage 4
Developing Thunderstorm

Focus:
- lightning
- wind
- stronger rainfall

---

## Stage 5
Mature Thunderstorm

Focus:
- severe weather
- hail
- downdrafts
- high instability

---

# Specialization Phase

At later stages, storms branch into specialization paths.

---

# Tornado Path

Focus:
- rotation
- concentrated destruction
- debris

Possible Evolutions:
- rope tornado
- cone tornado
- wedge tornado
- violent tornado

---

# Hurricane Path

Focus:
- size
- flooding
- sustained power

Possible Evolutions:
- tropical storm
- hurricane
- major hurricane
- hyper hurricane

---

# Blizzard Path

Focus:
- freezing
- snow
- visibility reduction

Possible Evolutions:
- snowstorm
- blizzard
- mega blizzard

---

# Lightning Storm Path

Focus:
- electrical discharge
- chain lightning
- atmospheric energy

Possible Evolutions:
- electrical storm
- superstorm
- plasma storm

---

# Future Expansion Paths

- dust storms

---

# Evolution Design Rules

Evolution should require:
- environmental conditions
- resource thresholds
- atmospheric instability

Storms should not evolve instantly.
